# THE PARALLAXIN CONSTITUTION

**Version 1.0 — Ratified before the first line of code**

## PREAMBLE

- Power speaks constantly. It speaks through press conferences, official statements, sanctions, treaties, and bombs. It speaks through silence — what it chooses not to say.

- Parallaxin exists because there is a measurable, documentable distance between what power says and what power does. That distance is not opinion. It is not ideology. It is geometry — observable from enough angles, with enough evidence, by anyone willing to look.

- This platform does not hold truth. It holds evidence. It does not deliver verdicts. It delivers the raw material from which any honest person can reach their own.

- We are not neutral. Neutrality is a political position that pretends equidistance between a documented fact and a convenient lie. We are objective — we follow evidence wherever it leads, regardless of who it serves or who it damages.

- We are a lens. Not an oracle. Not a judge. Not an activist. A lens.

## ARTICLE 1: PURPOSE

### 1.1 Parallaxin exists to document:

- In plain language and verifiable sources, the gap between the stated positions and the documented actions of powerful actors — governments, institutions, military forces, and individuals who command resources that affect human life at scale.

### 1.2 Parallaxin is not a news organization.

- It does not compete for speed. It competes for accuracy and completeness.

### 1.3 Parallaxin is not a political project.

- It has no position on how the world should be governed, which states should exist, or which ideologies are correct. It has one position only: that documented actions should be compared against stated claims, and the result should be available to every person on earth, free of charge, in their own language.

### 1.4 The primary audience is the citizen:

- The person whose life is affected by the decisions of powerful actors and who deserves access to the documented record of what those actors say versus what they do.

## ARTICLE 2: THE EVIDENCE STANDARD

> This is the spine of the platform. Everything else is scaffolding around it.

### 2.1 — Hierarchy of Sources

| Tier	| Description	| Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tier 1: Primary	| Official documents, transcripts, court records, international body reports (UN, IAEA, ICC, ICJ), verified satellite imagery, video/photo with confirmed geolocation and timestamp, financial disclosures	| Foundation of all claims |
| Tier 2: Corroborated	| Multiple independent outlets reporting the same fact with named sources; OSINT analysis with documented methodology; peer-reviewed academic research; leaked documents verified independently by 2+ outlets	| Sufficient for publication |
| Tier 3: Single-source	| One outlet, one named source, or anonymous source with circumstantial corroboration	| Published only with explicit label: "Single-source — awaiting corroboration" |
| Tier 4: Unverifiable	| Anonymous claims without corroboration, unverified social media, "sources say" without attribution, self-published claims by conflict parties without independent verification	| Excluded from all content |

### 2.2 No factual claim enters the platform without Tier 2 sourcing at minimum.

### 2.3 Tier 3 material may appear in Claim Autopsies as the claim being examined, never as established fact.

### 2.4 Tier 4 material does not appear on the platform in any context.

### 2.5 Every source is linked.Every link is archived. If a source disappears, the archived version is cited. If no archive exists, the claim is downgraded or "removed?"

### 2.6 This standard applies equally to all actors — allied, adversarial, or unaligned with any position. The evidence standard does not bend based on who the evidence concerns.

## ARTICLE 3: CONTENT PRINCIPLES

### 3.1 — Describe acts, not identities.

- Never: "terrorist," "freedom fighter," "regime," "moderate," "extremist"
- Always: specific descriptions of specific acts with specific dates and specific sources
"Armed group that carried out [attack] on [date] resulting in [documented casualties]"
"Government that authorized [operation] on [date] targeting [documented targets]"

### 3.2 — Separate fact from inference. Always. Visibly.

- [DOCUMENTED] — a verifiable fact with cited source
- [INFERENCE] — a reasoned conclusion drawn from documented facts, labeled as such
The reader must never wonder which is which

### 3.3 — Strong voice, not editorial voice.

- We do not editorialize. We do not tell the reader what to think. We arrange documented facts in sequence so that the weight is felt without instruction.

> Wrong: "Netanyahu cynically exploited the war to avoid his corruption trial."

> Wrong: "Some analysts suggest the war may have had domestic political benefits."

> Right: "Netanyahu's corruption trial was postponed indefinitely on [date], citing wartime emergency provisions. The trial had been scheduled to resume on [date]. His approval ratings rose from X% to Y% following the strikes. No Israeli Prime Minister has faced a completed corruption trial during wartime. [Sources]"

- The facts carry the weight. We arrange them honestly. The reader concludes.

### 3.4 — No framing by omission.

- Choosing where a story "begins" is an editorial act. Parallaxin documents the full chain of documented causation, not a politically convenient starting point. If an event has documented antecedents, those antecedents are included.

### 3.5 — "Code comments" for context.
Where historical context, analytical notes, or explanatory material is needed, it is visually and structurally distinct from factual documentation — like a developer's comment in source code. Informative. Never disguised as fact.

## ARTICLE 4: GOVERNANCE

### 4.1 — Evidence over authority.

- No individual — including the founder/s, any editor, any contributor regardless of seniority or volume of contributions — may override the Evidence Standard. The standard governs the platform. People serve it.

### 4.2 — Disputes are resolved by evidence, not consensus.

```
Step 1: A claim is challenged → challenger provides counter-evidence
Step 2: Both claims reviewed against primary sources
Step 3: If sources conflict → both documented with source quality rating
Step 4: If one source is clearly stronger → stronger source prevails
Step 5: If genuinely ambiguous → labeled "contested — see sources"
         Never resolved by vote. Never.
```

### 4.3 — All editorial decisions are public.

- Every content decision, every dispute, every resolution is documented in the public repository. No private channels, no back-room agreements, no editorial calls made off-record.


### 4.4 — No hierarchy of contributors.

- A first-time contributor's evidence is weighed identically to the founder's. There are no senior editors, no editorial board with override power, no reputation scores that confer authority. The evidence speaks. Nothing else.

## ARTICLE 5: ANTI-CAPTURE

- This article exists because every platform built on principles has eventually been tested by money, power, or popularity. We design for that test in advance.

### 5.1 — Financial independence.

| Acceptable | Unacceptable |
| --- | --- |
| Distributed crowdfunding where no single source exceeds 5% of total | Foundation grants with reporting requirements |
| Infrastructure donations with zero editorial conditions, publicly documented | Corporate sponsorship of any kind |
| Volunteer labor | Government funding from any government |
| Free-tier hosting services | Advertising of any kind |

### 5.2 All financial flows, if any ever exist, are publicly documented in the repository with the same transparency demanded of the actors the platform covers.

### 5.3 — Political independence.

- The platform has no national allegiance, no party affiliation, no ideological alignment. It documents actors of every nationality, ideology, and alignment by the same standard.

### 5.4 — Popularity independence.

- The platform does not optimize for traffic, engagement, shares, or virality. It optimizes for accuracy and completeness. If a documented fact is unpopular, it stays. If a popular narrative is unsupported by evidence, it is excluded or challenged.

### 5.5 — Capture detection.

- The Git repository is the immune system. Every edit, every deletion, every modification is permanently recorded. If capture is attempted — through systematic bias in edits, suppression of evidence, or manipulation of the dispute process — the history is visible to anyone. Capture cannot hide in a Git log.

## ARTICLE 6: THE FOUNDER CLAUSE

### 6.1 The individual who initiated this project holds no permanent authority over it.

### 6.2 Upon the first external contribution accepted via the standard process, the founder becomes one contributor among equals, subject to every article of this Constitution without exception.

### 6.3 The founder holds no veto power, no special editing rights, no privileged access to any system or decision.

### 6.4 The founder's contributions are reviewed by the same Evidence Standard as all others. The founder's disputes are resolved by the same process.

### 6.5 If the founder attempts to override this Constitution, that attempt is itself a documentable act — and the community's response is to fork, not to comply.

--

## ARTICLE 7: OPENNESS AND RESILIENCE

### 7.1 All code is open source (MIT License). All content is open access (Creative Commons Attribution 4.0).

### 7.2 The complete platform — code, content, and this Constitution — is forkable at any time by anyone. No technical, legal, or organizational mechanism exists to prevent forking. This is by design.

### 7.3 If the primary instance is compromised, captured, or taken down, any fork that adheres to this Constitution is a legitimate continuation. The platform is the principles, not the URL.

### 7.4 No user accounts are required to read any content. No personal data is collected from readers. No analytics track individual behavior. The platform serves the reader; it does not surveil the reader.

### 7.5 Contributions are made via public pull request with full Git attribution. Contributors may use pseudonyms. The evidence they provide is evaluated by the Evidence Standard, not by their identity.

--

## ARTICLE 8: LANGUAGE AND ACCESS

### 8.1 Every human being affected by the actions of powerful actors deserves access to the documented record in their own language.

### 8.2 English is the initial working language. Arabic and Farsi are immediate priorities given the pilot conflict's affected populations.

### 8.3 Translation is a community function, not a gatekept one. Any person may contribute a translation via the standard contribution process. Translations are reviewed for accuracy by other speakers, not by editors.

### 8.4 The technical architecture supports internationalization from day one — not as an afterthought, not as a feature request, but as a founding requirement.

--

## ARTICLE 9: WHAT WE ARE NOT

### 9.1 We are not a news agency. We do not chase breaking stories. We document patterns.

### 9.2 We are not activists. We do not advocate for policy, regime change, intervention, or withdrawal. We document what actors do and compare it to what they say.

### 9.3 We are not judges. We do not declare guilt or innocence. We present evidence in structured form and let the record speak.

### 9.4 We are not anonymous accusers. Every claim we present is sourced, linked, and verifiable by any reader independently. If we cannot source it, we do not publish it.

### 9.5 We are not combatants. Documenting a conflict is not participating in it. This principle is protected under international humanitarian law. We assert that protection explicitly.

--

## ARTICLE 10: AMENDMENT

### 10.1 This Constitution may be amended, but only by evidence-based argument documented publicly in the repository.

### 10.2 No amendment may weaken the Evidence Standard (Article 2), the Anti-Capture provisions (Article 5), or the Founder Clause (Article 6). These are immutable.

### 10.3 Proposed amendments are subject to a public comment period of no less than 30 days.

- Adoption requires demonstrated consensus among active contributors, defined as contributors with accepted contributions in the preceding 90 days.

### 10.4 If an amendment is disputed, it is resolved by the same evidence-based process as content disputes — not by vote of popularity, but by the strength of the argument for why the change better serves the platform's purpose.

- This document was written before the first line of code, before the first content record, before the first contributor joined. It was written first because it governs everything that follows.

The platform belongs to this document. Not to its founder. Not to its most prolific contributor. Not to its largest donor, because there will be none. Not to any government, because none are welcome to the table.

It belongs to anyone who believes that the distance between what power says and what power does should be measured, documented, and made visible to every person with an internet connection.
